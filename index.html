Björn Englesson

About

My name is Björn Englesson and I have a Bachelor in Computer Science and I am studying to become a Master of Science in Human Computer Interaction at KTH Royal Institute of Technology. Besides school I have been the project leader for the orientation of new Computer Science students at KTH Royal Institute of Technology. I am interested in virtual reality, augmented reality and user experience. I like challenges and am not afraid of throwing myself into new situations. I believe that is the best way of learning new things; learning by doing.

Projects

Glimpse

A school project in which I created an augmented reality application for iOS and Android which could look into virtual windows placed on real walls together with five other people. I was responsible for the AR part and putting together the scenes. The application was developed using Unity and Vuforia. A demo video can be seen here and our website can be found here.

Duration - 4 Weeks


Cauldron

A school project in which I developed a 2D platform game together with five other people. I was responsible for all game programming. The game was made in Unity with all scripting done in C#. A demo video can be seen here and our website can be found here.



The Chosen One

A school project in which the player gets to experience what it is like to be Neo from the Matrix and dodge incoming bullets. Created using Unreal Engine 4, the HTC Vive, and the Microsoft Kinect. The project was made by six people where I was responsible for the HTC Vive and Kinect integration together with one other group member. A demo video can be seen here and our website for our project can be found here.



VR Shooting Range Demo

A small project I developed over a weekend wanting to test how a shooting game would feel in a virtual reality environment. A demo video can be seen here.



God-like Third Person VR Concept

While creating the electrical wheelchair puzzle we also discussed creating a puzzle game from the perspective of a god looking down on a third person character. I wanted to try that concept out and created a very basic demo over a weekend consisting of the new perspective and a small level using assets from the wheelchair puzzle. A short video showing the demo can be seen here. This demo was created in Unreal Engine 4 using the HTC Vive as the VR headset.



Electrical Wheelchair VR Puzzle Demo

During the summer of 2016, my friends and I created a small virtual reality puzzle game for the HTC Vive. We were interested in how to approach the problem of movement in virtual reality so we decided to give a virtual electrical wheelchair a try. The puzzle game was created using Unreal Engine 4 and the HTC Vive. A video of the demo can be seen here.


Virtual Reality Blocks Toy

A school project in which I created a virtual version of a classic children's toy in which you have to place blocks of different shapes in different shaped holes. The project blog can be found here. This demo was created using Unity3D, the Leap Motion, and the Oculus Rift DK2.


Voice Controlled Game Companion

A school project in which I created a voice controlled companion together with three other students. In the demo the companion is able to respond to simple commands given vocally by the player. A demo video is available here. The demo was created using Unity3D and Microsoft's Cortana for the voice commands.


Tiny Flame

Tiny Flame is a simple game developed for iOS. The objective of the game is to help the tiny flame dodge falling raindrops. Try to obtain the highest score possible!

Currently not available on the App Store.
